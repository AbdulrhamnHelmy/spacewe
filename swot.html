The Space Weather Operational Readiness Development (SWORD) Center of Excellence

Focus: Satellites, orbital dynamics, and space debris SWORD is dedicated to studying how space weather affects satellites, orbital paths, and space debris management. Geomagnetic storms and solar wind conditions can alter spacecraft trajectories, increase atmospheric drag, and raise collision risks with debris. The center supports orbit correction planning, collision avoidance strategies, and the protection of space assets such as space stations and commercial satellite constellations like Starlink.
