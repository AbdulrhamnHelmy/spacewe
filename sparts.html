The Space Weather Research and Technology Applications (SPARTA) Center

Focus: Ionospheric disturbances and communications The SPARTA Center studies how space weather affects the Earth's ionosphere, vital for radio wave propagation and navigation accuracy. Solar flares, geomagnetic activity, and solar wind fluctuations can cause sudden ionospheric disturbances, leading to GPS errors, radio blackouts, and aviation communication issues. SPARTA develops high-resolution models simulating ionospheric density, electron content, and signal distortions. These models improve forecasting and early-warning systems for aviation, maritime transport, military, and emergency services. The center integrates data from radars, ionosondes, and satellites for real-time monitoring. A key mission is identifying how ionospheric storms impact HF radio, satellite communications, and aircraft navigation. Predicting disruptions helps reroute flights, adjust GPS systems, and protect critical infrastructure. The center collaborates with research institutions to refine models of total electron content (TEC), equatorial plasma irregularities, and auroral disturbances. It studies polar cap absorption events affecting high-latitude communication. SPARTA also develops technologies and protocols for communication resilience using AI, multi-source data fusion, and predictive models, aiming to reduce outages, navigation errors, and timing disruptions.
