Radiation and Geomagnetic Storms

They are high-speed charged particles that were triggered by solar eruptions.

High-speed particles from solar eruptions can sometimes: get past most of Earth’s magnetic defenses, following Earth’s magnetic field lines toward the north and south poles, where they enter our atmosphere and possibly even hit the ground (but don’t harm anyone on the ground). knock electrons off of atoms and molecules in our atmosphere (ionizing them), altering high-frequency radio communication. pierce deep into satellite hardware, degrading solar panels and damaging circuits. pass through human tissue, posing radiation risks to astronauts in space or to crewmembers and passengers in high-flying polar aircraft.

March 1989 Storm

March 1989 Storm – Quebec BlackoutLocation: Quebec, Canada.Event: A CME (Coronal Mass Ejection) shook Earth's magnetosphere.Impact:Power outage affecting 6 million people for 9 hours.Satellites damaged, communication systems disrupted.Lesson: Modern grids are highly vulnerable to solar storms.
