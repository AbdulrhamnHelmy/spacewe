The Center for All-Clear Solar Energetic Particle Forecasts (CLEAR)

Focus: High-energy solar particles The CLEAR Center plays a critical role in protecting human spaceflight and spacecraft systems from the dangers of solar energetic particles (SEPs). These particles are often released during solar flares and coronal mass ejections, traveling at high velocities that allow them to reach Earth and deep-space environments in a short time. The center studies how SEPs propagate through interplanetary space and how quickly they can reach spacecraft, lunar missions, and Martian transfer orbits. One of its primary goals is the development of real-time and near-real-time forecasting models capable of issuing early alerts before radiation exposure reaches harmful levels. This includes integrating satellite data, solar observatories, and heliophysics simulations to better predict the onset and trajectory of radiation storms. CLEAR also collaborates with mission planners to create shielding protocols and exposure response strategies for astronauts aboard missions like Artemis, the Lunar Gateway, and future Mars expeditions. The center further analyzes how radiation affects sensitive onboard systems such as avionics, communication modules, power supplies, and life-support components. It also contributes to defining safe operational windows and emergency shelter procedures during solar storms. In addition, CLEAR assists in shaping guidelines for spacecraft hardening and instrument calibration to prevent malfunctions caused by energetic particles. Another key area of research includes long-term radiation risk modeling, especially for prolonged missions outside Earth's magnetosphere. By combining data from previous missions, such as SOHO, Parker Solar Probe, and ACE, CLEAR helps refine predictions of SEP frequency and intensity during different solar cycles.
